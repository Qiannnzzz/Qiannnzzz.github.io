7-Latent feature extraction with a prior-based self-attention framework for spatial transcriptomics

genome research, Shengquan Chen nankai.edu.cn software-PAST
introduction: a prior-based self-attention framework for spatial transcriptomics, a variational graph convolutional autoencoder, integrates prior information via a Bayesian neural network, captures spatial patterns via a self-attention mechanism, and enables scalable application via a ripple walk sampler strategy.
features: past is the first ST method that integrates reference data to analyze ST data

8-Programs, Origins, and Niches of Immunomodulatory Myeloid Cells in Gliomas **

bioRvix Bradley E. Bernstein-Harvard Medical School
Data: 183k myeloid cells express four immunomodulatory activity programs
Scavenger Immunosuppressive, C1Q Immunosuppressive,CXCR4 Inflammatory, and IL1B Inflammatory All four programs are present in IDH1 mutant 
multi-modality: scRNA-seq data and mitochondrial DNA-based lineage tracing, spatial transcriptomics, and organoid explant systems 
result: these programs are driven by microenvironment and therapy, not cell type, origin, or mutant status.
C1Q Immunosuppressive program- routinely administered dexamethasone
Scavenger Immunosuppressive program includes ligands with established roles in T-cell suppression, are induced in hypoxic regions, and immunotherapy resistance.

9-Revealing Molecular Mechanisms of Early-Onset Tongue Cancer by Spatial Transcriptomics

bioRvix Evgeny V. Denisov-Russian National Research Center
methods: spatial whole transcriptome
obj: early tongue cancer compared with old one
finding:

  1. oxidative stress, vascular mimicry, MAPK, and JAK-STAT pathways,
  2. myeloid-derived suppressor cells, TAM, and plasma cells increased.
  3. the invasive front with vascular mimicry with the arrangement of TAM and aggregation of plasma cells and lymphocytes organized into a teritary lymphoid structure.
  4. Early-onset tongue cancer demonstrates extremely downregulated oxidative phosphorylation and upregulated glycolysis.
